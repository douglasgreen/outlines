## March

### Realtor Commission Settlement

The recent settlement by the National Association of Realtors (NAR) marks a significant change in
the real estate industry, particularly concerning the traditional 6% commission on home sales. Here
are the key points from the settlement:

#### Key Changes

1. **Elimination of the 6% Commission**:

    - The NAR agreed to a settlement that eliminates the longstanding practice of a 6% commission on
      home sales. This commission was typically split between the seller's broker and the buyer's
      agent.

2. **Decoupling of Commissions**:

    - The settlement introduces a "decoupling" of commissions, meaning buyers and sellers will now
      be responsible for paying their own agents. Sellers will no longer cover the buyer's agent
      fees, which were previously included in the home sale price.

3. **Impact on Buyers and Sellers**:

    - Buyers will have more control over how much they pay their agents, potentially negotiating
      fees or opting for different payment structures like flat fees or hourly rates.
    - Sellers will benefit from reduced costs, as they will no longer need to factor in the buyer's
      agent commission into the sale price.

4. **Potential Reduction in Home Prices**:

    - With the reduction in commission fees, home prices could decrease, making homeownership more
      accessible. The average savings on a home priced at $417,000 could be between $6,000 and
      $12,000.

5. **Market and Industry Impact**:

    - The settlement is expected to lead to a more competitive real estate market, with agents
      potentially offering lower fees to attract clients. This could also lead to a shift in how
      real estate transactions are conducted, with more transparency and competition.

6. **Legal and Industry Repercussions**:
    - The settlement follows a federal jury's finding that the NAR and some brokerages were liable
      for keeping commissions artificially high. This has led to significant legal and financial
      repercussions for the NAR, including a $418 million settlement.

#### Summary

The settlement by the NAR is poised to transform the real estate industry by eliminating the
traditional 6% commission structure. This change is expected to lower the costs of buying and
selling homes, increase market competition, and potentially reduce home prices. Both buyers and
sellers will have more flexibility and control over agent fees, which could lead to more
personalized and cost-effective real estate services.

### Baltimore Key Bridge Collapse

The collapse of the Baltimore Key Bridge occurred after a container ship, the Dali, lost power and
collided with the bridge. Here is a summary of the events and contributing factors:

#### Incident Overview

-   **Collision Details**: The Dali, a 985-foot-long cargo ship, lost power and struck one of the
    bridge's supports at a speed of 8 knots (approximately 9 mph). This impact caused the bridge to
    snap and fall into the river below.
-   **Timing**: The incident happened early Tuesday morning, around 1:30 a.m., which helped limit
    the number of vehicles on the bridge at the time.

#### Impact and Casualties

-   **Casualties**: Six construction workers who were filling potholes on the bridge were affected.
    Two were rescued, while the bodies of two others were recovered later. The remaining four
    workers are presumed dead.
-   **Ship and Crew**: The ship, owned by Singapore-based Grace Ocean Private Ltd., had 21 crew
    members and two pilots on board. All were accounted for, with no serious injuries reported.

#### Structural and Safety Factors

-   **Bridge Condition**: The Francis Scott Key Bridge was 47 years old and rated in fair condition.
    It lacked pier protection, which could have helped withstand such an impact.
-   **Design Flaws**: The bridge was "fracture critical," meaning it lacked redundancy. If one part
    failed, it could lead to a complete collapse, which is what happened in this case.

#### Environmental and Economic Impact

-   **Port Closure**: The collapse has shut down ship traffic at the Port of Baltimore, a major East
    Coast shipping hub, affecting cargo and commuter traffic.
-   **Hazardous Materials**: Some containers on the ship contained hazardous materials, but there
    was no immediate environmental threat detected.

#### Investigation and Response

-   **Investigation**: The National Transportation Safety Board (NTSB) is leading the investigation,
    which could take one to two years. They are examining both the ship's condition and the bridge's
    structural integrity.
-   **Government Response**: The Biden Administration approved $60 million in emergency federal aid
    for initial recovery efforts. President Biden has committed to covering the entire cost of
    rebuilding the bridge.

This incident highlights the importance of structural redundancy in bridge design and the potential
risks associated with large cargo ships navigating near critical infrastructure.

## May

### Trump Guilty Verdict

Former President Donald Trump was found guilty on all 34 felony counts of falsifying business
records in a New York state court. This conviction marks the first time a former or sitting U.S.
president has been convicted of criminal charges. The charges stem from Trump's efforts to conceal a
$130,000 hush money payment to adult film star Stormy Daniels, intended to influence the outcome of
the 2016 presidential election.

The jury, composed of 12 Manhattan residents, deliberated for over 11 hours before delivering the
unanimous verdict. The trial, which lasted nearly six weeks, included testimony from key witnesses
such as Michael Cohen, Trump's former attorney, who testified about the reimbursement scheme for the
hush money payment.

Despite the conviction, legal experts suggest that Trump is unlikely to face a prison sentence, as
he is a first-time offender. Each count carries a potential penalty of up to four years in prison
and/or a fine of up to $5,000, but defendants typically receive sentences well below the maximum.

Trump's legal team plans to appeal the verdict, arguing that the trial's timing and location were
unfair and that the jury was biased. The sentencing is scheduled for July 11, just days before the
Republican National Convention, where Trump is expected to be nominated as the party's presidential
candidate for the 2024 election.

## July

### 2024 Butler, Pennsylvania Shooting

On July 13, 2024, an assassination attempt was made on former President Donald Trump during a
campaign rally at the Butler Farm Show Grounds near Butler, Pennsylvania. The shooter, Thomas
Matthew Crooks, a 20-year-old from Bethel Park, Pennsylvania, fired eight rounds from an AR-15-style
rifle from the roof of a nearby building. Trump was injured in the upper right ear, and one audience
member was killed, with two others critically injured. Crooks was shot and killed by the Secret
Service's Counter Sniper Team shortly after he began firing.

#### Key Details

-   **Security Failures**: The incident highlighted significant security lapses by the Secret
    Service, described as the most significant failure since the attempted assassination of
    President Ronald Reagan in 1981. There was a lack of clear leadership and communication among
    the security teams, and critical security measures were not implemented.
-   **Response and Aftermath**: Trump was quickly taken to a hospital, treated, and released the
    same day. He appeared at the Republican National Convention in Milwaukee two days later, visibly
    wearing a bandage on his ear.
-   **Political Reactions**: The shooting prompted widespread condemnation from political leaders,
    including President Joe Biden, who called for an independent review of the security arrangements
    and emphasized the need for peaceful political discourse.
-   **Security Reforms**: Following the incident, there were calls for increased security measures
    for major political candidates, including the use of bulletproof glass at outdoor rallies. The
    Secret Service faced bipartisan criticism, leading to the resignation of its director, Kimberly
    Cheatle.

This event underscored the ongoing security challenges faced by political figures and the need for
robust protective measures to prevent such incidents in the future.

#### Biden Withdrawal

Joe Biden announced his withdrawal from the 2024 United States presidential election on July

poorly received debate performance against Donald Trump on June 27, 2024. Biden's performance raised

increasingly favored a new candidate to challenge Trump.

### 2024 Summer Olympics

The 2024 Summer Olympics, officially known as the Games of the XXXIII Olympiad, were held in Paris,
France, from July 26 to August 11, 2024. This marked the third time Paris hosted the Summer
Olympics, having previously done so in 1900 and 1924. The opening ceremony was unique as it took
place along the Seine River, making it the first time in Olympic history that the ceremony was not
held inside a stadium.

#### Key Highlights

-   **Participation**: Approximately 10,500 athletes from 206 countries participated in the Games.
    Notably, the 2024 Olympics were the first to feature an equal number of male and female
    athletes.
-   **Events and Sports**: The Games included 329 medal events across 45 sports. Four sports were
    newly introduced or reintroduced: breaking (breakdancing), skateboarding, sport climbing, and
    surfing. Breaking made its Olympic debut, while the other three appeared for the second time
    after their introduction in the 2020 Tokyo Olympics.
-   **Venues**: Events were held in 17 different cities across metropolitan France and Tahiti. The
    Stade de France in Saint-Denis served as the main Olympic Stadium. The surfing competition took
    place in Teahupo'o, Tahiti, located 9,765 miles from Paris.
-   **Medal Table**: The United States topped the medal table with 40 golds and a total of 126
    medals. China followed with 40 golds and 91 total medals, while Japan secured third place with
    20 golds and 45 total medals.
-   **Theme and Mascot**: The official theme song was "Parade," and the motto was "Games Wide Open"
    (French: Ouvrons grand les Jeux), emphasizing inclusivity and equality. The mascot was the
    Olympic Phryge, inspired by the Phrygian cap, a symbol of freedom in France.

Overall, the 2024 Paris Olympics were celebrated for their inclusivity, innovative opening ceremony,
and the introduction of new sports, reflecting a modern and diverse approach to the Olympic Games.

### Hurricane Helene

Hurricane Helene was a significant and catastrophic tropical cyclone that struck the southeastern
United States in late September 2024. Here's a summary of its key aspects:

#### Formation and Development

-   **Origin**: Helene began as a region of scattered thunderstorms in the western Caribbean Sea on
    September 23, 2024. It quickly organized and was classified as Tropical Storm Helene by the
    National Hurricane Center.
-   **Rapid Intensification**: The storm rapidly intensified due to abnormally warm waters in the
    Caribbean Sea and Gulf of Mexico, attributed to global warming. It transformed from a tropical
    storm to a Category 4 hurricane in just over two days.

#### Landfall and Impact

-   **Landfall**: Helene made landfall in northwestern Florida on September 26, 2024, as a Category
    4 hurricane with winds of 140 mph and a storm surge of 15 feet in some areas.
-   **Path**: After hitting Florida, Helene moved inland, affecting Georgia, Tennessee, North
    Carolina, and other southeastern states with heavy rains and strong winds.

#### Damage and Casualties

-   **Fatalities**: The hurricane resulted in over 230 deaths, primarily due to flooding, making it
    the deadliest U.S. hurricane since Hurricane Maria in 2017.
-   **Economic Impact**: The storm caused extensive damage estimated at $200 billion, potentially
    making it the costliest storm in U.S. history.
-   **Infrastructure Damage**: Helene's impact included widespread flooding, road washouts, power
    outages affecting millions, and significant structural damage across multiple states.

#### Rescue and Relief Efforts

-   **Emergency Response**: The U.S. National Guard and various rescue teams conducted operations to
    save trapped individuals. Relief efforts were complicated by misinformation on social media.
-   **Recovery**: Efforts to restore power and clear debris began shortly after the storm, with
    federal and state agencies coordinating relief supplies and financial resources.

#### Aftermath and Climate Considerations

-   **Climate Change**: Helene's rapid intensification and severity were seen as evidence of climate
    change's impact on hurricane strength. The Gulf of Mexico's warming trend has been noted as a
    contributing factor.
-   **Record Rainfall**: The storm brought record-breaking rainfall, particularly in the
    southeastern U.S., exacerbating flooding and causing long-term environmental impacts.

Hurricane Helene serves as a stark reminder of the increasing intensity of tropical cyclones in the
context of global climate change, highlighting the need for improved preparedness and response
strategies.

## October

### Hurricane Milton

Hurricane Milton was a significant and destructive Category 5 Atlantic hurricane that occurred in
October 2024. It was the thirteenth named storm and the second Category 5 hurricane of the 2024
Atlantic hurricane season. Milton became the second-most intense Atlantic hurricane ever recorded
over the Gulf of Mexico, following Hurricane Rita in 2005.

#### Meteorological History

-   **Formation and Intensification**: Milton formed from a tropical disturbance in the western
    Caribbean Sea and consolidated in the Bay of Campeche on October 5, 2024. It underwent explosive
    intensification, reaching Category 5 status with peak winds of 180 mph and a pressure of 897
    mbar, making it the fifth-most intense Atlantic hurricane on record.
-   **Path and Landfall**: The hurricane weakened to a Category 3 before making landfall near Siesta
    Key, Florida, on October 9, 2024. It caused significant damage across Florida, including tornado
    outbreaks and flooding, before becoming extratropical on October 10 and dissipating on
    October 13.

#### Impact

-   **Casualties and Damage**: Hurricane Milton resulted in at least 35 fatalities, with 32 in the
    United States and 3 in Mexico. The storm caused over $85 billion in damages, making it one of
    the costliest hurricanes.
-   **Affected Areas**: The hurricane impacted Mexico's Gulf Coast, the northern Yucatán Peninsula,
    Western Cuba, the Southeastern United States (especially Florida and Georgia), and the Bahamas.
-   **Tornadoes and Flooding**: Milton spawned numerous tornadoes and caused widespread flooding,
    particularly in Florida, where rainfall amounts reached up to 15 inches in some areas.

#### Climate Change Influence

-   Scientists attributed the rapid intensification of Milton to abnormally high sea surface
    temperatures, which were made more probable by climate change. The hurricane's wind speeds and
    rainfall were also intensified due to climate change effects.

Overall, Hurricane Milton was a devastating storm that highlighted the increasing intensity of
hurricanes potentially linked to climate change. The storm's rapid intensification and widespread
impact underscore the need for improved preparedness and response strategies in hurricane-prone
regions.

### Death of Liam Payne

Liam Payne, a former member of the popular boy band One Direction, tragically died at the age of 31
after falling from a hotel balcony in Buenos Aires, Argentina. The incident occurred at the Casa Sur
Hotel in the Palermo neighborhood, where Payne was staying. Local police reported that Payne fell
from the third floor, resulting in fatal injuries.

Before the fall, hotel staff had called emergency services, expressing concerns about Payne's
behavior, which was reportedly influenced by drugs and alcohol. The hotel manager mentioned that
Payne was destroying his room and there was fear due to the presence of a balcony. An autopsy
confirmed that Payne had traces of alcohol, cocaine, and a prescription antidepressant in his
system, but the fall was determined to be accidental, not a deliberate act.

Payne's death has led to legal actions in Argentina, with three individuals charged in connection
with the incident. These charges include abandonment of a person followed by death and supplying
narcotics. The investigation revealed that Payne was likely not fully conscious at the time of his
fall.

Liam Payne rose to fame as part of One Direction, which was formed on the British TV show "The X
Factor" in 2010. The band became one of the best-selling boy bands globally before going on an
indefinite hiatus in 2016. Payne had been open about his struggles with substance abuse and mental
health issues throughout his career. He is survived by his son, Bear, whom he had with former
partner Cheryl Tweedy.

## November

### 2024 United States presidential election results

The 2024 United States presidential election resulted in Donald Trump being elected as the 47th
president, marking a significant political comeback after his defeat in 2020. Trump, running with
vice-presidential candidate JD Vance, defeated the Democratic ticket of Kamala Harris and Tim Walz.
Trump secured 312 electoral votes compared to Harris's 226, flipping key swing states such as
Arizona, Georgia, Michigan, Nevada, Pennsylvania, and Wisconsin.

#### Key Points

-   **Electoral College and Popular Vote**: Trump won 312 electoral votes, surpassing the 270 needed
    to win. He also won the popular vote with 49.8%, totaling 77,284,118 votes, while Harris
    received 48.3% with 74,999,166 votes.
-   **Voter Turnout**: The 2024 election saw a high voter turnout of 63.9%, with over 156 million
    Americans voting. This was slightly lower than the 66.6% turnout in 2020 but still significant
    by modern standards.
-   **Congressional Results**: Republicans gained control of both the House and Senate. They secured
    220 seats in the House and 53 in the Senate, flipping four Senate seats in Montana, Ohio,
    Pennsylvania, and West Virginia.
-   **Demographics and Representation**: The 119th Congress will have 150 women, with a slight
    decrease in the number of women of color compared to the previous Congress. Notably, the Senate
    will have two Black women serving simultaneously for the first time in history.

#### Summary

Donald Trump's victory in the 2024 election marks a historic return to the presidency, making him
the first president since Grover Cleveland to serve non-consecutive terms. His campaign focused on
issues such as the economy, healthcare, and immigration, resonating with working-class voters and
improving his support among young men and Hispanic voters. Despite the victory, the election
highlighted the continued political division in the United States, with a narrow margin in both the
popular and electoral votes.

Luigi Mangione, a 26-year-old suspect, has been charged with the murder of UnitedHealthcare CEO
Brian Thompson. The incident occurred on December 4, 2024, in Midtown Manhattan. Mangione was
arrested on December 9 in Altoona, Pennsylvania, after being recognized by a McDonald's employee. He
was found with a ghost gun, a suppressor, and multiple fake IDs.

The murder has been classified as an act of terrorism, with Mangione facing multiple charges,
including murder in the second degree as a crime of terrorism, weapons charges, and forgery.
Evidence against him includes a gun matching the one used in the shooting, shell casings with
specific inscriptions, and his fingerprints on items found at the crime scene.

Mangione's motive appears to be linked to his disdain for the health insurance industry, as
indicated by a handwritten manifesto found in his possession. This document criticized the
industry's practices and expressed anger towards corporate greed. Despite his arrest, Mangione has
not entered a plea and is being held without bail. His family has expressed shock and devastation
over the incident.
