## AT&T Outage

The AT&T service outage of 2024 was a significant event that affected a large number of customers
across the United States. Here are the key details:

-   **Date and Duration**: The outage occurred on February 22, 2024, starting at 3:30 AM Eastern
    time and lasted for over twelve hours. It was one of the most widespread outages since a major
    T-Mobile outage in June 2020.
-   **Impact**: Customers were unable to make calls, send texts, or access the internet. This
    disruption had a significant impact on communication, especially for those relying solely on
    their smartphones. Emergency services were also affected, with many local 911 centers issuing
    alerts to use alternative methods to reach 911.
-   **Cause**: AT&T attributed the outage to "the application and execution of an incorrect process"
    during a network expansion. This was understood to be a human error related to software updates
    rather than a cyberattack, despite initial speculations about terrorism or solar flares.
-   **Response and Resolution**: AT&T was slow to acknowledge the problem, making a public
    announcement only by noon Eastern time. The service was fully restored more than twelve hours
    after the outage began. The company clarified that the outage was not due to a cyberattack.
-   **Comparison to Past Events**: The outage was reminiscent of a similar incident in the 1990s
    when an upgrade to the signaling system caused a nationwide disruption. The modern centralized
    network, while more efficient, is more susceptible to widespread outages due to human error
    during updates.
-   **Subsequent Issues**: In June 2024, AT&T experienced another outage due to an interoperability
    issue between carriers, which was resolved after collaboration with other carriers. This
    incident also affected 911 services in some areas, although AT&T stated that 911 calls generally
    went through.

Overall, the 2024 AT&T outage highlighted the vulnerabilities in modern telecommunications networks,
particularly the risks associated with centralized software updates and the importance of robust
testing procedures to prevent such widespread disruptions.

## Baltimore Key Bridge Collapse

The collapse of the Baltimore Key Bridge occurred after a container ship, the Dali, lost power and
collided with the bridge. Here is a summary of the events and contributing factors:

### Incident Overview

-   **Collision Details**: The Dali, a 985-foot-long cargo ship, lost power and struck one of the
    bridge's supports at a speed of 8 knots (approximately 9 mph). This impact caused the bridge to
    snap and fall into the river below.
-   **Timing**: The incident happened early Tuesday morning, around 1:30 a.m., which helped limit
    the number of vehicles on the bridge at the time.

### Impact and Casualties

-   **Casualties**: Six construction workers who were filling potholes on the bridge were affected.
    Two were rescued, while the bodies of two others were recovered later. The remaining four
    workers are presumed dead.
-   **Ship and Crew**: The ship, owned by Singapore-based Grace Ocean Private Ltd., had 21 crew
    members and two pilots on board. All were accounted for, with no serious injuries reported.

### Structural and Safety Factors

-   **Bridge Condition**: The Francis Scott Key Bridge was 47 years old and rated in fair condition.
    It lacked pier protection, which could have helped withstand such an impact.
-   **Design Flaws**: The bridge was "fracture critical," meaning it lacked redundancy. If one part
    failed, it could lead to a complete collapse, which is what happened in this case.

### Environmental and Economic Impact

-   **Port Closure**: The collapse has shut down ship traffic at the Port of Baltimore, a major East
    Coast shipping hub, affecting cargo and commuter traffic.
-   **Hazardous Materials**: Some containers on the ship contained hazardous materials, but there
    was no immediate environmental threat detected.

### Investigation and Response

-   **Investigation**: The National Transportation Safety Board (NTSB) is leading the investigation,
    which could take one to two years. They are examining both the ship's condition and the bridge's
    structural integrity.
-   **Government Response**: The Biden Administration approved $60 million in emergency federal aid
    for initial recovery efforts. President Biden has committed to covering the entire cost of
    rebuilding the bridge.

This incident highlights the importance of structural redundancy in bridge design and the potential
risks associated with large cargo ships navigating near critical infrastructure.

unnecessary regulations, and restructure federal agencies to increase efficiency.

### Key Objectives and Functions

-   **Purpose**: DOGE's primary goal is to cut federal expenditures and improve government
    efficiency by reducing waste and abolishing redundant agencies. It aims to achieve significant
    budget cuts, potentially up to $2 trillion, by downsizing the federal workforce and
    consolidating federal agencies.
-   **Leadership and Structure**: The commission is led by Elon Musk and Vivek Ramaswamy, with other
    members like Katie Miller. It operates outside the formal government structure and does not have
    regulatory powers but can influence policy through recommendations.
-   **Proposed Actions**: DOGE plans to focus on regulatory rescissions, administrative reductions,
    and cost savings. It may recommend eliminating certain federal agencies and reducing the number
    of federal employees significantly.

### Legislative and Political Context

-   **Congressional Support**: A congressional caucus and subcommittee have been formed to support
    DOGE's mission. The Delivering Outstanding Government Efficiency Caucus and the Delivering on
    Government Efficiency Subcommittee will work closely with DOGE.
-   **Challenges and Criticisms**: The commission faces skepticism regarding its ability to achieve
    its ambitious goals. Critics argue that the proposed budget cuts are unrealistic and could harm
    essential government functions. Concerns about potential conflicts of interest have also been

### Lifespan and Future Plans

-   **Expiration Date**: DOGE is set to conclude its work by July 4, 2026, coinciding with the

-   **Reception**: The initiative has received mixed reactions, with some supporting the idea of

## Earth's Core Slowing

Recent research has confirmed that Earth's inner core, a solid iron sphere located deep within the
planet, has slowed its rotation to the point where it is now moving backward relative to the rest of
the Earth. This phenomenon is part of a decades-long cycle of speeding up and slowing down, which
has been a subject of scientific debate for many years.

### Key Points

-   **Independent Rotation**: The Earth's inner core rotates independently of the planet's surface.
    This rotation has been studied since its discovery by Danish seismologist Inge Lehmann in 1936.
-   **Seismic Wave Analysis**: Scientists use seismic waves from earthquakes to study the core's
    movement. By analyzing these waves, researchers have deduced changes in the core's position and
    spin over time.
-   **70-Year Cycle**: The core's rotation follows a 70-year cycle, involving periods of speeding up
    and slowing down. This cycle was confirmed by recent studies that analyzed seismic data from
    earthquakes and nuclear tests.
-   **Backward Movement**: The inner core was previously spinning faster than the Earth, a
    phenomenon known as super-rotation. However, it has now slowed down and is moving backward
    relative to the mantle.
-   **Impact on Earth's Dynamics**: The core's movement influences Earth's magnetic field, which
    protects the planet from solar radiation. Changes in the core's rotation could potentially
    affect the magnetic field and slightly alter the length of a day.
-   **Scientific Debate**: While the recent findings have provided compelling evidence for the
    core's slowing and reversing rotation, the study of the core remains challenging due to its
    inaccessibility and the limited data available.
-   **Future Research**: Scientists emphasize the need for more data and improved interdisciplinary
    tools to further explore the core's dynamics and its interactions with the outer core and
    mantle.

### Implications

The changes in the core's rotation are subtle and have a minimal impact on daily life, affecting the
length of a day by only milliseconds. However, understanding these dynamics is crucial for
comprehending Earth's geophysical behavior and the processes occurring deep within the planet.
Further research is needed to unravel the complexities of the core's interactions with other layers
of the Earth.

## El Niño Ending

The El Niño event of 2023-2024, which began in June 2023, was one of the strongest on record and had
significant global impacts. It contributed to record-breaking temperatures both in the air and
oceans, with every month from June 2023 to May 2024 being the hottest on record. This El Niño event
influenced various weather patterns, leading to extreme conditions such as droughts in northern
South America and severe drought in parts of Africa, as well as excessive rainfall in regions like
Kenya and California.

By early 2024, the El Niño was starting to weaken, with scientists predicting its conclusion by late
spring. This weakening was expected to transition into a neutral phase before potentially shifting
to La Niña conditions later in the year. The transition from El Niño to La Niña is significant
because it typically results in different global weather patterns, such as increased hurricane
activity in the Atlantic due to reduced wind shear.

The end of El Niño and the potential onset of La Niña are expected to influence weather patterns
significantly. For instance, La Niña summers following strong El Niño winters are often among the
hottest on record in the U.S., with above-average temperatures anticipated across much of the
country. Additionally, the absence of El Niño's moderating effects on Atlantic hurricanes could lead
to a more active hurricane season.

Overall, the 2023-2024 El Niño had a profound impact on global weather, contributing to extreme

## European Artificial Intelligence Act

The European Artificial Intelligence Act of 2024 is a pioneering legal framework designed to
regulate artificial intelligence across Europe. It aims to ensure the safe and ethical development
and deployment of AI technologies while fostering innovation and maintaining trust among users.

### Key Objectives

-   **Risk Management**: The AI Act introduces a risk-based approach, categorizing AI systems into
    four levels of risk: minimal, limited, high, and unacceptable. This classification helps in
    applying appropriate regulatory measures based on the potential impact of AI systems.
-   **Trustworthy AI**: The Act emphasizes the importance of AI systems respecting fundamental
    rights, safety, and ethical principles. It aims to build trust by ensuring transparency and
    accountability in AI operations.
-   **Support for Innovation**: While setting clear requirements for AI developers and deployers,
    the Act also seeks to reduce administrative and financial burdens, particularly for small and
    medium-sized enterprises (SMEs).

### Risk-Based Approach

-   **Minimal Risk**: AI systems like spam filters and video games face no obligations but can
    voluntarily adhere to codes of conduct.
-   **Limited Risk**: Systems with transparency risks, such as chatbots, must inform users they are
    interacting with AI. AI-generated content must be clearly labeled.
-   **High Risk**: AI systems used in critical areas like healthcare and recruitment must comply
    with strict requirements, including risk mitigation and human oversight.
-   **Unacceptable Risk**: AI applications that pose a clear threat to fundamental rights, such as
    social scoring, are banned.

### Implementation and Enforcement

-   The European AI Office oversees the enforcement and implementation of the AI Act, ensuring
    compliance and fostering collaboration among stakeholders.
-   The Act includes provisions for ongoing quality and risk management, adapting to technological
    changes to remain future-proof.

### Timeline

-   The AI Act entered into force on August 1, 2024, with full applicability expected in two years.
    Specific provisions, such as prohibitions and governance rules, have staggered implementation
    timelines.

Overall, the EU's AI Act of 2024 positions Europe as a leader in ethical AI development, balancing
innovation with the protection of fundamental rights and safety.

## Realtor Commission Settlement

The recent settlement by the National Association of Realtors (NAR) marks a significant change in
the real estate industry, particularly concerning the traditional 6% commission on home sales. Here
are the key points from the settlement:

### Key Changes

1. **Elimination of the 6% Commission**:

    - The NAR agreed to a settlement that eliminates the longstanding practice of a 6% commission on
      home sales. This commission was typically split between the seller's broker and the buyer's
      agent.

2. **Decoupling of Commissions**:

    - The settlement introduces a "decoupling" of commissions, meaning buyers and sellers will now
      be responsible for paying their own agents. Sellers will no longer cover the buyer's agent
      fees, which were previously included in the home sale price.

3. **Impact on Buyers and Sellers**:

    - Buyers will have more control over how much they pay their agents, potentially negotiating
      fees or opting for different payment structures like flat fees or hourly rates.
    - Sellers will benefit from reduced costs, as they will no longer need to factor in the buyer's
      agent commission into the sale price.

4. **Potential Reduction in Home Prices**:

    - With the reduction in commission fees, home prices could decrease, making homeownership more
      accessible. The average savings on a home priced at $417,000 could be between $6,000 and
      $12,000.

5. **Market and Industry Impact**:

    - The settlement is expected to lead to a more competitive real estate market, with agents
      potentially offering lower fees to attract clients. This could also lead to a shift in how
      real estate transactions are conducted, with more transparency and competition.

6. **Legal and Industry Repercussions**:
    - The settlement follows a federal jury's finding that the NAR and some brokerages were liable
      for keeping commissions artificially high. This has led to significant legal and financial
      repercussions for the NAR, including a $418 million settlement.

### Summary

The settlement by the NAR is poised to transform the real estate industry by eliminating the
traditional 6% commission structure. This change is expected to lower the costs of buying and
selling homes, increase market competition, and potentially reduce home prices. Both buyers and
sellers will have more flexibility and control over agent fees, which could lead to more

## Solar Eclipse

The total solar eclipse of April 8, 2024, also known as the "Great North American Eclipse," was a
significant astronomical event visible across parts of North America, including Mexico, the United
States, and Canada. Here's a summary of the event:

### Path and Visibility

-   **Path of Totality**: The eclipse began over the South Pacific Ocean and first reached land on
    Mexico's Pacific coast. It then traveled northeast through the United States, passing through
    states such as Texas, Oklahoma, Arkansas, Missouri, Illinois, Kentucky, Indiana, Ohio,
    Pennsylvania, New York, Vermont, New Hampshire, and Maine. The eclipse continued into Canada,
    crossing Southern Ontario, Quebec, New Brunswick, Prince Edward Island, and Nova Scotia, before
    exiting North America on the Atlantic coast of Newfoundland.
-   **Totality Duration**: The longest duration of totality was approximately 4 minutes and 28
    seconds, occurring near Nazas, Durango, Mexico.
-   **Population Impact**: Approximately 44 million people lived within the path of totality, with
    an estimated 50 million experiencing the total eclipse when including those who traveled to view
    it. Additionally, about 652 million people witnessed a partial eclipse.

### Scientific and Public Engagement

-   **Scientific Opportunities**: The eclipse provided unique opportunities to study the Sun's
    corona, which is usually obscured by the Sun's bright surface. NASA and other scientific
    organizations conducted various studies and public engagement activities to enhance
    understanding of solar phenomena.
-   **Public Viewing**: Safety was emphasized for viewing the eclipse, with recommendations for
    using specialized solar viewing glasses or indirect viewing methods during the partial phases.
    The total phase allowed for safe direct viewing without protective eyewear.

### Historical Context

-   This eclipse was the first total solar eclipse visible from Canada since 2008 and the first from
    the contiguous United States since 2017. It was also the only total solar eclipse in the 21st
    century visible from all three countries: Mexico, the United States, and Canada.

The event was a remarkable opportunity for both scientific study and public enjoyment, drawing
millions of viewers across North America.

## UEFA Euro 2024

UEFA Euro 2024 was the 17th edition of the UEFA European Football Championship, held in Germany from
June 14 to July 14, 2024. This tournament marked the third time European Championship matches were
played in Germany, but the first time in what was formerly East Germany, with Leipzig as a host
city. The tournament returned to its usual four-year cycle after the 2020 edition was postponed due
to the COVID-19 pandemic.

### Key Details

-   **Host Country:** Germany
-   **Dates:** June 14 – July 14, 2024
-   **Teams:** 24
-   **Venues:** 10 cities across Germany, including Berlin, Munich, and Leipzig
-   **Champion:** Spain, winning their fourth title
-   **Runner-up:** England
-   **Total Matches Played:** 51
-   **Total Goals Scored:** 117 (2.29 per match)
-   **Total Attendance:** 2,681,288 (52,574 per match)
-   **Top Scorers:** Harry Kane, Georges Mikautadze, Jamal Musiala, Cody Gakpo, Ivan Schranz, Dani
    Olmo (3 goals each)
-   **Best Player:** Rodri
-   **Best Young Player:** Lamine Yamal

### Tournament Highlights

-   **Group Stage:** Germany started strong with a 5-1 victory over Hungary. Spain and England also
    performed well, with Spain not conceding any goals in the group stage.
-   **Knockout Stage:** Spain defeated Germany in the quarter-finals and went on to win the
    tournament by beating England 2-1 in the final. England reached the final despite a challenging

    resilience by making comebacks in several matches.

## UnitedHealthcare CEO Shooting

Luigi Mangione, a 26-year-old suspect, has been charged with the murder of UnitedHealthcare CEO
Brian Thompson. The incident occurred on December 4, 2024, in Midtown Manhattan. Mangione was
arrested on December 9 in Altoona, Pennsylvania, after being recognized by a McDonald's employee. He
was found with a ghost gun, a suppressor, and multiple fake IDs.

The murder has been classified as an act of terrorism, with Mangione facing multiple charges,
including murder in the second degree as a crime of terrorism, weapons charges, and forgery.
Evidence against him includes a gun matching the one used in the shooting, shell casings with
specific inscriptions, and his fingerprints on items found at the crime scene.

Mangione's motive appears to be linked to his disdain for the health insurance industry, as
indicated by a handwritten manifesto found in his possession. This document criticized the
industry's practices and expressed anger towards corporate greed. Despite his arrest, Mangione has
not entered a plea and is being held without bail. His family has expressed shock and devastation
over the incident.

## United States Election Results

The 2024 United States presidential election resulted in Donald Trump being elected as the 47th
president, marking a significant political comeback after his defeat in 2020. Trump, running with
vice-presidential candidate JD Vance, defeated the Democratic ticket of Kamala Harris and Tim Walz.
Trump secured 312 electoral votes compared to Harris's 226, flipping key swing states such as
Arizona, Georgia, Michigan, Nevada, Pennsylvania, and Wisconsin.

### Key Points

-   **Electoral College and Popular Vote**: Trump won 312 electoral votes, surpassing the 270 needed
    to win. He also won the popular vote with 49.8%, totaling 77,284,118 votes, while Harris
    received 48.3% with 74,999,166 votes.
-   **Voter Turnout**: The 2024 election saw a high voter turnout of 63.9%, with over 156 million
    Americans voting. This was slightly lower than the 66.6% turnout in 2020 but still significant
    by modern standards.
-   **Congressional Results**: Republicans gained control of both the House and Senate. They secured
    220 seats in the House and 53 in the Senate, flipping four Senate seats in Montana, Ohio,
    Pennsylvania, and West Virginia.
-   **Demographics and Representation**: The 119th Congress will have 150 women, with a slight
    decrease in the number of women of color compared to the previous Congress. Notably, the Senate
    will have two Black women serving simultaneously for the first time in history.

### Summary

Donald Trump's victory in the 2024 election marks a historic return to the presidency, making him
the first president since Grover Cleveland to serve non-consecutive terms. His campaign focused on
issues such as the economy, healthcare, and immigration, resonating with working-class voters and
improving his support among young men and Hispanic voters. Despite the victory, the election
highlighted the continued political division in the United States, with a narrow margin in both the
popular and electoral votes.

## Yoon Suk Yeol Impeachment

President Yoon Suk Yeol of South Korea was impeached on December 14, 2024, following a controversial
and short-lived declaration of martial law. This decision marked a significant constitutional crisis
in South Korea, reminiscent of its past military dictatorships. The impeachment was driven by public
outrage and political opposition, as Yoon's martial law decree was seen as an overreach of power and
a response to domestic political pressures rather than external threats.

### Key Events Leading to Impeachment

-   **Martial Law Declaration**: On December 3, 2024, President Yoon declared martial law, citing
    threats from "North Korea's communist forces" and the need to eliminate "anti-state elements."
    This move was perceived as a reaction to internal scandals and political pressure rather than a
    genuine national security threat.
-   **Immediate Backlash**: The declaration lasted only a few hours before being blocked by the
    National Assembly. Lawmakers, some of whom had to physically force their way into the assembly,
    voted to reverse the martial law decision.
-   **Political Turmoil**: Following the reversal, protests erupted across Seoul, demanding Yoon's
    resignation. The defense minister, Kim Yong-hyun, who was instrumental in the martial law plan,
    offered his resignation amid calls for his impeachment as well.
-   **Impeachment Vote**: The opposition-dominated National Assembly moved quickly to impeach Yoon,
    making him the third South Korean president to be suspended through such a vote. This was
    followed by the impeachment of Prime Minister Han Duck-soo, who was acting as president after
    Yoon's suspension.

### Consequences and Political Climate

-   **Leadership Crisis**: The impeachment of both the president and the acting president created
    unprecedented political instability in South Korea. Choi Sang-mok, the finance minister, was
    appointed as the new acting president.
-   **Public Reaction**: The public's response was marked by large-scale protests and a demand for
    accountability, reflecting deep-seated frustrations with Yoon's administration and its handling
    of power.
-   **Criminal Inquiry**: In addition to impeachment, Yoon faces a criminal investigation, marking
    the first time a sitting South Korean president has been subjected to such scrutiny.

This series of events underscores the fragility of political stability in South Korea and highlights
the challenges of maintaining democratic norms in the face of executive overreach.

## Wisconsin School Shooting

The Madison, Wisconsin school shooting of 2024 occurred at Abundant Life Christian School on
December 16. The shooter, identified as 15-year-old Natalie Rupnow, who also went by Samantha, was a
freshman student at the school. The tragic event resulted in the deaths of a teenage student and a
substitute teacher, with Rupnow dying from a self-inflicted gunshot wound. Six others were injured,
including two students with life-threatening injuries and a teacher and three students with
non-life-threatening injuries.

The shooting took place in a study hall classroom on the second floor of the school. The shooter
used a handgun, and although two firearms were found at the scene, only one was used in the attack.
The motive for the shooting is still under investigation, with authorities examining Rupnow's social
media activity and relationships.

The incident prompted a swift response from law enforcement, with officers arriving at the scene
within minutes of the first 911 call made by a second-grade teacher. The Madison Police Department
and other agencies are working to trace the origin of the gun used in the shooting.

The community and local leaders have expressed their grief and called for action to prevent such
tragedies. President Joe Biden and Wisconsin Governor Tony Evers both emphasized the need for
stronger gun control measures and mental health support. The school, which serves around 420
students from kindergarten through high school, has closed early for the Christmas holiday in the
wake of the shooting.
